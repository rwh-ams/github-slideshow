Participant Information Sheet

The research project and this study

This study forms part of a UK-wide research project which is investigating flood resilience in urban areas. The data collected in this study will be used to explore implicitly held associations with regards to perceptions of rainwater harvesting. We are further interested in whether results from implicit association tests match those obtained in self-reporting measures (surveys, questionnaires, etc.).

Rainwater harvesting can contribute to reducing pressure on urban drainage infrastructure with respect to flood mitigation but uptake of such systems in the UK is generally low. Understanding public perceptions of rainwater harvesting systems is critical to understanding the lack of implementation.

What is involved?

We will ask you to complete three short tests, each of which last 3-4 minutes. Participating in the study should take no longer than 10-12 minutes of your time. All three tests involve studying sets of images and answering a series of questions about them. In the third test, the Implicit Association Test (IAT), we record how quickly you respond to the images and a series of negative and positive words. We are interested in how quickly you make associations between the two.

We have identified no risks associated with participating in this study. Please note that participation is entirely voluntary, no monetary reward is being offered for taking part.

Your identifying information

  • The survey is anonymous and there is the option to provide demographic information at the end.
  • Any information that could identify you will be removed, or replaced with codes, before files are shared or results made public.
  • The University may store your data for up to 25 years and for a period of no less than 7 years after the research project finishes. The researchers who gathered or processed the data may also store the data indefinitely and reuse it in future research.
  • All data will be stored in folders on the UoN OneDrive. Microsoft OneDrive is an ISO 27001 information security management compliant service that allows secure and controlled sharing of data amongst the research team by encrypting data both in transit and at rest and is approved against the University’s Handling Restricted Data Policy.
  • Extracts of data provided may be disclosed in published works that are posted online for use by the scientific community. It may also move with the researcher who collected your data to another institution in the future.
